Maximizing Shopify Speed: A Case Study on Custom Liquid Optimization

Published on Jan 15, 2026 • 8 min read

In the world of e-commerce, every millisecond counts. Research shows that a 1-second delay in page load time can lead to a 7% reduction in conversions. This case study details how I took a struggling Shopify store with a desktop speed score of 45 and optimized it to a staggering 98.

The Challenge

A client approached me with a store that felt sluggish. They were using a premium theme but had installed dozens of apps, each injecting their own scripts. The homepage was heavy with unoptimized images and redundant Liquid loops that were firing multiple times.

The Solution

  • Script Audit: Removed 5 redundant apps and replaced their functionality with custom Liquid code, reducing external JS requests by 40%.
  • Lazy Loading: Implemented native browser lazy loading for all images below the fold and used "fetchpriority" for the LCP hero image.
  • Liquid Refactoring: Optimized nested "for" loops in the product grid to reduce server-side execution time.
  • Critical CSS: Extracted core layout styles to be inlined in the header, ensuring the store feels fast even before the full stylesheet loads.

The Results

Post-optimization, the store saw a 12% increase in average time on site and a 4.5% lift in conversion rate within the first month. The Google PageSpeed Insights score stabilized at 98/100 for desktop and 92/100 for mobile.
